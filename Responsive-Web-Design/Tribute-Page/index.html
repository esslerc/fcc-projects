Frederick Banting and Charles Best

100 years of insulin

Sir Frederick Grant Banting (November 14, 1891 - February 21, 1941) was a Canadian medical scientist.

Charles Herbert Best (February 27, 1899 - March 31, 1978) was an American-Canadian medical scientist.

In 1921, Banting and Best isolated pancreatic extracts from dogs, which was the first version of a viable type of insulin for people with diabetes. This research was the beginning of saving the lives of millions of diabetics. Before the discovery of insulin, the lives of people with diabetes were cut short. While a diabetes diagnosis can be life-altering, it is no longer a death sentence.
